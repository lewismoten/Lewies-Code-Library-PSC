Overview

Over the many years, I had posted small pieces of code onto the internet. This was primarily done so that I would have a centralized location to access my files from anywhere. The best explination I can think of is that this was more like a personal hard drive where the public had read-only access.

Unfortunately, I got a little famouse along the way. Not majorly, but I would receive a ton of e-mail with questions. I even got a new job during the .com craze where they came to me. Well, the fame has dies down and I have survived the dot com crash.

I have formed a noble value along the way ... the ability to teach. To make little scripts public for others to learn from - as long as it is not proprietary information that is sensitive to my employer or my clients. So you will not find any robust applications here that you can sell off or compete with.
